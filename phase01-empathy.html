Phase 01 · Empathy

Understand users before you solve.

In this phase, your team interviews, observes, listens and documents real user needs before moving to Define.

What is Empathy?

Empathy is the first phase of Design Thinking. Students go out into the real world to understand the people they are designing for — their feelings, struggles and real needs.

What students need to do

  • Prepare interview questions using T01 Interview Guide.
  • Conduct interviews with at least 3 real target users.
  • Record and transcribe interviews with permission.
  • Complete Empathy Map, Persona and User Needs Summary.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Do not ask leading questions such as “Don’t you think this is a problem?”
  • Do not interview only friends. Find real target users.
  • Do not assume what users feel without evidence.
  • Do not skip observation. Watch what users actually do.

T01 Interview Guide + Evidence

Prepare questions, ask consent, conduct the interview, record if allowed and capture transcript evidence.

Not Started
Template Intro

Purpose of this template

This template guides students to collect real user evidence. It combines interview questions, consent, recording, transcript, observations and early insights in one place.

InputT00 preparation
ActionInterview + observe
OutputTranscript + 3 insights

A1Correct interview sequence

Before: prepare warm-up, main questions and probing starters.

Start: introduce the team and explain the project purpose.

Consent: ask permission before recording; use user code, not real name.

During: listen actively and ask follow-up questions.

After: transcribe key responses and write 3 insights immediately.

Sample Interview Guide

Canteen peak-hour interview

Weak question

“Do you agree the canteen needs an app?”

Better question

“Tell me about your experience buying lunch during peak hour.”

Opening script: “Thank you for helping us. We want to understand your real lunch experience. There is no right or wrong answer.”
Consent script: “Can we record this short conversation only for project notes? We will not use your real name.”
✍️ Fill guide

Warm-up: start easy. Main questions: ask experience-based questions. Insights: write what you learned, not your solution.

Consent Reminder

Ask permission before recording. Avoid sensitive personal information and use interviewee codes.

T02 Empathy Map

Organise one user's evidence into Says, Thinks, Does, Feels, Pains and Gains.

Not Started
Template Intro

Purpose of this template

The Empathy Map helps students separate direct quotes, observed actions, possible thoughts and emotions. This prevents teams from jumping to solutions too early.

InputTranscript + observations
ActionSort evidence
OutputEmpathy Map

A2What students need to do

Says: use exact user quotes.

Does: use observed behaviour.

Thinks/Feels: infer carefully based on evidence.

Pains/Gains: summarise struggles and desired benefits.

Sample Empathy Map

U01 — Canteen user

Says

“The queue is too long.”

Thinks

“I might miss class.”

Does

Checks the time repeatedly.

Feels

Frustrated and rushed.

Reminder: Says and Does need direct evidence. Thinks and Feels must be reasonable interpretations.

A4What students need to do

Sample Needs Summary

From evidence to user need

Pattern

Students complain about long queues during short breaks.

Evidence

3 users mentioned waiting time and fear of being late to class.

User Need

Students need a faster way to decide where to buy lunch because they have limited break time.

Important: Do not write the final solution yet. Write what the user needs and why.
