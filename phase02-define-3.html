What is Define?

Define is where your team makes sense of everything learned during Empathy. You analyse interviews, maps and personas to frame ONE clear problem statement that captures the real user need.

What students need to do

  • Review Empathy Map, Persona and User Needs Summary from Phase 01.
  • Identify repeated patterns across all interviews.
  • Write a Point of View statement using [user] needs [need] because [insight].
  • Generate 5 How Might We questions to prepare for Ideation.
  • Submit for Supervisor Gate 1 before starting Phase 03.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Do not include the solution inside the problem statement.
  • Do not make the scope too broad or too narrow.
  • Do not jump straight into brainstorming before HMW.
  • Do not base the problem on assumptions. Use research evidence.

What is this template for?

A persona is not a real person — it is a summary of the patterns you saw across your Empathy interviews and observations, given a name and a face so your team can design for a real need instead of a guess.

InputPhase 01 evidence
FormulaRole + Goals + Frustrations

Open T05 Full Guide

A6What students need to do

1. Review your evidence: reread your T01–T04 notes, quotes and empathy map.

2. Find the pattern: choose the most common type of user, not one unusual case.

3. Give them a name and role: keep it realistic and specific to your project context.

4. List goals and frustrations: base every line on real evidence, not assumption.

5. Add one quote: use real or realistic words that capture their feeling.

Sample Persona

Aina, the First-Time Visitor

Role

A Diploma student visiting the batik workshop for the first time with her class.

Goals

Wants to try batik-making and enjoy the experience without feeling embarrassed.

Frustrations

Afraid of spoiling the cloth and unsure of the correct steps to follow.

Key quote: "I was scared I'd spoil the cloth, so I just watched instead of trying."
Why this works: Every line traces back to real T02/T03 interview evidence, not a guess about who the user might be.

Template Intro

What is this template for?

An insight explains WHY a need matters, based on evidence that repeats across your users. The need statement is the one sentence your team carries forward into Ideation — it names a need, not a solution.

InputPersona + evidence
FormulaPattern → Insight → Need
OutputOne need statement
Open T06 Full Guide

A7What students need to do

1. Find the repeated pattern: what showed up across more than one interview or observation?

2. Write the insight: explain why this pattern matters to the user.

3. Write the need statement: use a verb such as to know, to feel, to access or to reduce.

4. Check it is a need, not a solution: "needs an app" is a solution; "needs reassurance" is a need.

Insight → Need Formula

1PatternWhat repeats?
2InsightWhy does it matter?
3NeedWhat must be met?
Sample Insight & Need

Reassurance before trying

Pattern

Several first-time visitors hesitated or only watched instead of joining in.

Insight

Because they were afraid of damaging the cloth and did not know the correct steps.

Need

The first-time visitor needs reassurance and clear step-by-step guidance before trying batik.

Why this works: The need is written as a feeling/action the user requires, not as an app, poster or product.

Use your Phase 01 evidence and T05 persona. Your need statement should be one short sentence.
