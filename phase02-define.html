Phase 02 · Define

Turn empathy evidence into a clear problem.

In this phase, your team reviews research findings, identifies patterns, writes a user-centred problem statement and creates HMW questions before moving to Ideation.

What is Define?

Define is where your team makes sense of everything learned during Empathy. You analyse interviews, maps and personas to frame ONE clear problem statement that captures the real user need.

What students need to do

  • Review Empathy Map, Persona and User Needs Summary from Phase 01.
  • Identify repeated patterns across all interviews.
  • Write a Point of View statement using [user] needs [need] because [insight].
  • Generate 5 How Might We questions to prepare for Ideation.
  • Submit for Supervisor Gate 1 before starting Phase 03.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Do not include the solution inside the problem statement.
  • Do not make the scope too broad or too narrow.
  • Do not jump straight into brainstorming before HMW.
  • Do not base the problem on assumptions. Use research evidence.

T05 Problem Statement

Turn your Empathy evidence into ONE clear, user-centred Point of View problem statement.

Start Here
Template Intro

What is this template for?

This template helps your team move from many Empathy findings into one focused problem. You are not proposing a solution yet. You are deciding what real user need your project should address.

InputEmpathy evidence
FormulaUser + need + insight
OutputOne POV statement

A5What students need to do

1. Review evidence: look at quotes, observations, pains and gains from Phase 01.

2. Find a repeated pattern: choose an issue that appears across users, not only one complaint.

3. Write the user need: use a verb-led need such as to know, to access, to decide or to reduce.

4. Add the insight: explain why the need matters based on real evidence.

5. Check your statement: it must define the problem only. Do not include the solution yet.

POV Formula

1UserWho has the problem?
2NeedWhat do they need to do?
3InsightWhy does it matter?
Sample POV Statement

Canteen peak-hour problem

User

A Diploma student at POLIPD who buys lunch during peak hour.

Need

Needs to know the canteen crowd level before leaving class.

Insight

Because walking to a full canteen wastes their limited lunch break and makes them late for class.

Complete POV: A Diploma student at POLIPD who buys lunch during peak hour needs to know the canteen crowd level before leaving class because walking to a full canteen wastes their limited lunch break and makes them late for class.
Why this works: It focuses on the user, states a need, and explains the insight without suggesting an app, QR code or final product.
✍️ Fill guide

Use your own Phase 01 evidence. Your answer should be short, clear and based on user research.

Who is facing the problem? Be specific about the user group.

What does the user need to do, know, access, reduce or improve?

Why does this need matter? Use repeated findings from interviews or observation.

Combine user, need and insight into one complete sentence.

What your project will focus on.

What your project will not address.

Explain the impact if this problem is not solved.

✓Problem Statement Check

It focuses on the user, not the team or technology.

It does not include a solution.

It is based on evidence from Phase 01.

It is clear enough to guide Ideation.

T06 How Might We Questions

Reframe your T05 problem statement into opportunity questions for Ideation.

Not Started
Template Intro

What is this template for?

HMW questions help your team move from problem framing into idea generation. A good HMW is open enough to create many ideas, but focused enough to stay connected to your user need.

InputT05 POV statement
Minimum5 HMW questions
OutputBest HMW selected

A6What students need to do

1. Copy your POV: bring your final problem statement from T05.

2. Write five HMW questions: start each one with “How might we…”

3. Keep it open: avoid mentioning one fixed solution such as app, QR code or chatbot too early.

4. Choose the best HMW: pick the question that can generate the strongest ideas in Phase 03.

Sample HMW Transformation

From POV to HMW

POV: A Diploma student at POLIPD who buys lunch during peak hour needs to know the canteen crowd level before leaving class because walking to a full canteen wastes their limited lunch break.
Weak HMW

How might we create an app for the canteen?

Better HMW

How might we help students decide where to buy lunch before the peak-hour rush?

Why better? It focuses on the user need and leaves room for many possible solutions.
